Banned Books Week

From bannedbooksweek.org:

Banned Books Week is an annual event celebrating the freedom to read. Banned Books Week was launched in 1982 in response to a sudden surge in the number of challenges to books in schools, bookstores and libraries. Typically held during the last week of September, it highlights the value of free and open access to information. Banned Books Week brings together the entire book community — librarians, booksellers, publishers, journalists, teachers, and readers of all types — in shared support of the freedom to seek and to express ideas, even those some consider unorthodox or unpopular.

For Banned Books Week, NCSSM will be promoting books that have been historically censored in the library.

Black History Month

Black History Month is an annual celebration of the achievements of African Americans in the United States. For Black History Month, the library will be featuring prominent African American authors such as Toni Morrison, Ralph Ellison, and Alice Walker, among others.
